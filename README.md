### 📌 Overview

**Cosmos Explorer** is a premium 3D space visualizer that lets you navigate the universe using only your hands. By stripping away traditional HUD clutter and utilizing a **pure-gesture interface**, it provides an unhindered view of planetary systems, asteroid belts, and celestial trajectories. Powered by **MediaPipe Hands** and **Three.js**, it identifies your right-hand landmarks in real-time to provide "perfect" fluid control.

---

### ✨ Key Features

*   🌍 **Pure Core Interface** – All text elements and HUD clutter have been removed for a cinematic, first-person exploration experience.
*   🦾 **Right-Hand Precision** – Optimized specifically for right-hand tracking, filtering out background noise for reliable control.
*   🌫️ **Holographic Skeleton** – A stylized digital "Neural Link" overlay represents your hand on-screen in real-time.
*   🌊 **Fluid Momentum** – Advanced Exponential Moving Average (EMA) smoothing for organic, jitter-free camera movement.
*   🛸 **Stealth Cam** – Camera video is processed in the background and hidden from the UI to maintain total immersion.
*   ☄️ **Interactive Orbitals** – Manage planetary rings, asteroid belts, and deep-space probes with natural movements.
*   ✨ **Neon Reticle** – A pulsing, minimalist targeter that follows your index finger for object inspection.

### 🖐️ Gesture Command Guide

Master the cosmos with these specialized right-hand gestures:

| Gesture | Action | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| ✋ **Open Palm** | **ORBIT** | Move your hand to rotate the scene and view planets from any angle. |
| 🤏 **Pinch** | **ZOOM** | Pinch fingers together or pull apart to zoom the telescope in and out. |
| ☝️ **Point** | **INSPECT** | Aim your index finger to target celestial objects with the digital reticle. |
| ✊ **Closed Fist** | **RESET** | Instantly return the camera to the default mission home position. |
| ✌️ **Two Fingers** | **TOGGLE** | Show or hide the asteroid belt with a peace sign gesture. |
| 🤙 **Shaka** | **VIEW** | "Hang loose" to cycle through different cinematic camera presets. |
